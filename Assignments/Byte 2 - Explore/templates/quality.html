Quality: About This Data Set

Since the two aspect of this 2015-2016 dataset we focus on - highest degree and location of the schools accredited by SACSCOC are hard to verified against with information online. So we compare them with data in 2018 and hand checked with a small sample of schools with highest degrees from their websites. Overall the data seems to be in good quality and with some minor concerns that we will discuss in more details below.

Is the data complete?

If not, what is missing and what concerns does it raise? What is your evidence for this?

The dataset gives 857 intitutions accredited by Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges(SACSCOC). And this dataset seems to include every school accredited by this association, since I have checked all the school that were accredited by this association in 2018 from this link: http://www.sacscoc.org/search.asp (clicked on "search all school"). And there are 794 insitutions. There is no information online about how many institutions were accredited by this association in 2015. So we can only conclude that, assuming the number of institutions accredited by SACSCOC does not change much each year, the dataset seems to be complete since the number of schools from this dataset is roughly the same as the number on official record of year 2018.

Conerns that could be raised if this dataset is incomplete is that we could be missing schools that are outside of states that SACSCOC claims to be in charge of.

Is the data coherent?

Does it make sense relative to itself? Does it match your expectations? Are the distributions sensible? What is your evidence for this?

Yes it does make sense since most of the schools are in the seven states SACSCOC claims to in charge of. If we take a look at the map, most of the schools accredited by SACSCOC are within the eleven states (Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas and Virginia) they are in charge of. But there are three of them in Ohio, Michigan, Arizona.

Is the data correct?

What aspects of this data can you verify? How did you do so? What did you find?

As mentioned above, I couldn't find a list of institutions accredited by SACSCOC in 2015, so I searched the three institutions in their database (http://www.sacscoc.org/search.asp) of this year, but none of them showed up in the search result. There could be two explanations: one is that the dataset is correct in including those three school in 2015-2016 and they are not accredited by SACSCOC in 2018; the other one is that the dataset is incorrect and they shouldn't be included.

For the degree offered by each school, there is no information available on SACSCOC and I didn't find a complete dataset with such informatoin online. As a rsult, I hand checked that with several school's websites and they are correct. Whether the dataset is correct about HIGHDEG is not comepletely verified.
